More Info

The Taj Mahal, located in Agra, India, was commissioned in 1632 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal. An ivory-white marble mausoleum, it is recognized as one of the greatest architectural achievements in history and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
